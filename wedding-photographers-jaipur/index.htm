A Walk Through The Brief History and Culture of Jaipur

Located in the eastern part of Rajasthan, a city drenched in magnificent forts, towering palaces, rich heritage, and brightly hued colors, the royal town of Jaipur in Rajasthan is India’s first planned city.

Ranging from the pink-painted sandstone buildings nestling throughout the cityscape that gives the name Pink City to the fantastic attractions that draw tourists from all over the world, it is very easy to see why Jaipur is considered to be one of the prime locations for destination weddings in India. The rich cultural heritage that is on display throughout this city of Rajasthan featuring traditions, customs, lifestyle, art, and architecture makes this place one of the best places to get married in India.

One can very well attain the best understanding of Jaipur through its art, music, and architecture along with the splendid monuments that have been sprinkled throughout the city like God’s own creation.

The huge fortresses and expansive palaces that take hours back to the reigns of Mughal and Rajput empires ranging from the Jal Mahal and Amber Fort to the pristine Hawa Mahal and Jantar Mantar make this city an architectural spectacle in all its glory.

The colorful outfits and unique jewelry of Jaipur along with the unparalleled warm ambiance and gestures of the local people make the capital city of Rajasthan not only visit-worthy but also wedding-worthy in India.

The thought of sauntering down the palatial aisle draped in a magnificent bridal outfit with the culturally rich and vibrant ceremonial backdrops of Jaipur while Rajasthani folk music reverberates throughout the palace makes the city naturally inclined towards becoming the prime destination wedding hub. It was founded by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, a Kachwaha Rajput, in the year 1727 and hence it is known for all the Rajputana-styled forts and palaces which enable to-be married couples to tie the knot and start new beginnings.

The groovy tunes of Rajasthani folk songs that are played with traditional instruments like Sarangi, Ektara, and Jhalar along with the famous dancers including Ghoomar and Chari where dancers dance on a pot with a lit earth lamp on their head, the entire culture and heritage atmosphere of this Pink City is a sight to behold for our wedding photographers in Jaipur.

Jaipur is also known for their carved silver jewelry, Kundan as well as meenakari jewelry, ivory carved sculptures, woodwork, and leather goods along with some of the locally made delicacy like delicious mangodi, papad, khichdi, buttermilk, and sohan halwa.

The capital city of Rajasthan also witnesses various fairs and festivals throughout the year which is nothing short of a color burst for professional wedding photographers in India who love to capture couples in front of diversifying backdrops. The people of Jaipur are friendly and warm and the colorful outfits and ethnic jewelry adds to the charm of the city, and all of these factors come up to make Jaipur is one of the best locations for Indian destination weddings.

Wedding Culture of Jaipur

Rajasthan is known worldwide for its excellent display of traditional weddings which is mostly associated with royalty and heritage. Rangeelo Rajasthan is full of colorfulness and tradition and a proper Rajasthani marriage consists of a lot of pre and post-Marwari wedding rituals.

Belonging to the Marwar region of Rajasthan, the Marwari wedding is the most common type of traditional wedding in Rajasthan apart from destination weddings which comprise traditional and modern weddings from all over the world. Known for rich and colorful customs, traditions, and rituals Marwari weddings are also very extravagant, lavish, and ornate affairs that last for several days.

Weddings in Jaipur are colorful and traditional marriages that depict the vibrant culture and tradition of Rajasthan. While people associate royalty with Rajasthani weddings, it is easy to see why traditional weddings in Rajasthan are grand affairs in terms of style, traditions, cultural display, and grandeur.

Our Jaipur wedding photographers love to capture the traditional display of vibrance and heritage that a typical Rajasthani wedding entails. Along with all the royal traditions a traditional Rajasthani wedding is also a vibrant display of colorful and bright attire along with traditional jewelry and folk entertainment.

Rajasthan has become one of the most favorite places to host a great wedding atmosphere with Rajasthani traditional wedding rituals and customs for couples across the globe who loves Indian heritage and culture. Ranging from Sagai or the engagement ceremony which takes place a couple of days before the actual wedding day to some of the most fascinating rituals like Ashwahrohan where the bride puts her foot on the grinding stone which symbolizes courage and Sindoordaan ceremony where the groom fills the parting of the bride’s hair with Sindoor, a traditional Rajasthani wedding is filled with interesting rituals and customs that are loved and celebrated by our destination wedding photographers in Jaipur.

From the luscious Rajasthani food that is served as wedding menus comprising of traditional Rajasthani dishes like Dal Bati Churma, Mohan Thal, Mawe Ki Kachori, Mirchi Bada to the popular Rajasthani traditional dresses like Ghagra and Choli and Rajasthani Poshak, any invitation to a Rajasthani wedding will be an unparalleled experience for a first-timer and if you are a lover of Indian tradition and culture, tying the knot in Jaipur, the heart of Rajasthan can be the best decision you will ever make.

Places To Get Married in Jaipur

Considered to be the top wedding destination in India, Jaipur is an architecturally opulent capital city of Rajasthan as it is repleted with options of amazing wedding venues that can make each and every wedding celebration an unforgettable affair.

If you want to host your fairytale destination wedding in Jaipur, read on to find the best wedding venues of the Pink City that can weave an out worldly sophistication for your new beginning.

1. Chomu Palace

Considered to be one of the best places to host your wedding celebration in Jaipur, the classic Chomu palace is a heritage hotel that can make your dream wedding a star-striking reality.

Featuring magnificent architectural beauty flora and fauna the main courtyard of this classic wedding venue of Jaipur is painted with scenes from Indian Mythology, which can also act as the main ceremonial backdrop for a memorable wedding photography session in Jaipur. Other wedding event spaces of this top Jaipur wedding venue are the Darbar Hall, Sheesh Mahal, Moti Mahal, Janana Courtyard, Manohar Garden, and Swimming Pool Baradari to host all your wedding celebrations in Jaipur within a wedding budget of 50 to 70 lakhs for up to 150 guests.

2. Rambagh Palace

Considered to be one of the most luxurious hotels in India, the prestigious Rambagh Palace in Jaipur comes from the house of the esteemed Taj Hotels. Set in 47 acres of landscaped gardens and a tranquil atmosphere, this destination wedding spot in Jaipur exudes elegance and the regal feel in its 79 rooms which are meant for guest accommodations, and 10 other wedding event spaces for organizing wedding ceremonies and other celebrations.

With a whopping capacity of a thousand guests, tying the knot in Rambagh Palace can cost you anywhere between 90 lakhs to 1.2 crores.

3. Le Meridien

If you want a luxurious destination wedding in Jaipur, this iconic hotel in the heart of Rajasthan can be your ultimate dream come true. This hotel wedding venue in Jaipur is strategically located and offers amazing banquet areas like Diwan-E-Khas, which is suitable for engagements or pre-wedding ceremonies; and Amer Ballroom, perfect for sangeet nights or reception parties.

The luxurious rooms for guest accommodations, banquet halls, and sprawling lawns make up for one of the most well-suited spots for Jaipur destination weddings. The total cost of a wedding at Le Meridien in Jaipur can range anywhere between 90 lakhs and 1.5 crores.

4. Fairmont Jaipur

Nestled among the sprawling Aravali Hills, this opulent hotel wedding venue in Jaipur with over 200 rooms and banquet halls makes it a perfect venue for a luxurious destination wedding in Rajasthan for up to 500 guests.

If you are considering hosting a wedding that is a class apart, Hotel Fairmont in Jaipur can bring you a themed wedding which can ensure to be interesting and hassle-free, where all you have to do is just book your wedding in Jaipur with them and then sit back and watch them pull off a grand celebration that you and your guests will not be able to forget. The total cost of a wedding at Fairmont, Jaipur is estimated at 90 lakhs to 1.5 crores.

5. Jai Mahal Palace

Owned by the Taj group of hotels, this heritage property has made its place among highly coveted people in the pursuit of class apart events. This 70-year-old architectural masterpiece of Rajasthan is home to some of the most sophisticated weddings in India that are steeped with luxury and grandeur. Divide it into 6 different wedding event spaces, this top Jaipur wedding venue for destination weddings offers you top-notch arrangements to make your big day special.

Featuring some of the most recognized wedding event spaces like the Durbar Hall and Lotus Pond, this iconic destination wedding spot in Jaipur is a romantic wedding venue that is suitable for large gatherings. The total cost of your wedding at Jai Mahal Palace can range anywhere between 90 lakhs and 1.5 crores.
